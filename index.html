New Mexico Resonance

About Us

New Mexico Resonance was incorporated as a nonprofit research corporation in 1997 to use magnetic resonance to study mainly fluids, their environments, and their motion. This means studying flows not only of liquids but of gases and solid particles as well as their mixtures. We also develop new NMR methodologies, where appropriate. We carry out such studies with funding from US government agencies such as National Science Foundation, Department of Energy, NASA, Air Force Office of Scientific Research, and NIH, as well as national labs and universities and we also rely on private donations. We interact with several universities especially with University of New Mexico where most of the scientists have adjunct professorships. We have a strong postdoctoral fellows program and a visiting scientist program. Most collaborations with universities involve graduate or undergraduate students and several students have conducted their thesis projects in our laboratory.

Our Mission

To lead Nuclear Magnetic Resonance into the future.
