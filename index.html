New Mexico Resonance


About Us

New Mexico Resonance is a nonprofit research organization dedicated to advancing applied magnetic resonance technologies for real-world challenges. Originally founded in 1997 to explore complex fluid and material systems using nuclear magnetic resonance (NMR), the organization has evolved to concentrate its expertise on targeted sensing solutions with direct impact in agriculture and environmental monitoring.

Today, New Mexico Resonance is focused on the development of a specialized NMR-based detection system for identifying wheat stem sawfly infestations—an agricultural pest responsible for significant crop losses. By combining precision instrumentation, innovative data processing, and technological R&D, we aim to deliver practical tools that enable earlier detection, improved crop management, and reduced economic impact for growers.

Operating as a small, research-driven nonprofit, New Mexico Resonance prioritizes technical innovation, collaboration with domain experts, and the translation of advanced physics into deployable field technologies.


Our Mission

To lead Nuclear Magnetic Resonance into the future by exploring and developing unique, non-traditional NMR systems and applications.
